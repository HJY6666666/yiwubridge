Transparent sourcing costs

The Truth About Yiwu Sourcing Agent Fees: How to Avoid Hidden Scams

"Free sourcing" and "0% commission" sound attractive, but experienced importers know that nobody can manage supplier search, quality control, warehousing, and shipping for free.

If a sourcing agent promises zero fees, the cost may appear somewhere else: hidden product markups, kickbacks from factories, inflated domestic delivery, unclear exchange rates, or expensive shipping quotes. A professional Yiwu sourcing agent should explain exactly how they charge before you place an order.

To help you source with peace of mind, here is how legitimate Yiwu agents usually charge and how to spot warning signs before you send money.

The standard industry fee structures

Professional sourcing agencies in China generally operate with one of three transparent pricing models.

  • Percentage commission: The most common model. The agent charges a clear service fee, often 3% to 5% of the total order value, for sourcing, quotation comparison, supplier follow-up, QC support, warehousing, and logistics coordination.
  • Fixed project fee: Used for highly specialized sourcing tasks, supplier audits, product development, or projects that require deep research before an order is confirmed.
  • Hourly or daily rate: Usually used when buyers physically visit Yiwu and need an agent to accompany them through the market, translate, shortlist suppliers, and negotiate on-site.

Why a transparent 3% to 5% fee can save you money

A clear commission aligns the agent's interest with yours. The agent's job is to help you find reliable suppliers, negotiate practical prices, prevent quality problems, and reduce shipping mistakes. You know the service cost before the order starts, so there is less room for hidden charges.

With a transparent fee model from YiwuGo Agent, you can see the real supplier price, compare quotations, and understand where your money goes. This is safer than accepting a "free" service where the margin is hidden inside product price or logistics.

Real supplier prices and better quality control

A trustworthy sourcing agent should be willing to show clear pricing from the market booth or factory whenever possible. That means you can understand the real product cost in RMB, the service fee, domestic delivery, packing costs, and shipping estimate.

Transparency also protects quality. If the agent earns money from you, not from supplier kickbacks, the QC team can be objective. If a batch of home furniture, storage products, utility trolleys, gifts, or daily-use items fails inspection, your agent should push the supplier to rework, replace, or remake the goods before export.

Common warning signs of hidden sourcing scams

  • The agent refuses to explain how they make money.
  • The quotation has only one total price and no product, packing, or shipping breakdown.
  • The supplier name, factory location, or market booth information is always hidden.
  • The shipping cost changes suddenly without a clear reason.
  • The agent says inspection is "not necessary" for a new supplier or customized product.
  • The product price looks unusually low, but the final landed cost is unclear.
